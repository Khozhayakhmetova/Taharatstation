Problem

Public facilities often rely on running taps for washing. Continuous flow is hard to control, leads to unnecessary water waste, and creates hygiene and maintenance issues.

Uncontrolled flow

Water runs continuously even when not needed, especially in high-traffic wash zones.

No measurement

Facilities rarely track water used per wash cycle, making savings hard to prove.

Operational burden

More water use can mean more cleaning, more slip risk, and higher utility costs.

Solution

A retrofit that delivers water as a controlled micro-spray instead of continuous flow. Designed for fast installation and ongoing maintenance through a hardware + service model.

Retrofit-friendly

Installs next to existing sinks using standard plumbing connections, minimizing downtime.

Controlled delivery

Dosed micro-spray targets ultra-low liters per wash cycle while maintaining usability.

Auditable KPI

Liters saved per cycle → converts into m³ saved per site/year for WRM reporting.

Impact

We report savings through a simple, auditable metric: liters saved per wash cycle, then translate to m³ saved per site per year based on measured usage.

Core KPI

~0.98 L saved per cycle (baseline 1.0 L/cycle vs target 0.02 L/cycle; pilot-validated target).

Scenario reporting

m³/year per site calculated for 50 / 200 / 500 cycles per day (transparent assumptions).

SDG alignment

Supports SDG 6 by improving water-use efficiency in public infrastructure (demand-side).
